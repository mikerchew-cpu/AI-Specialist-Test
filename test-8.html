2.1 Nickel Mining
Prompt 31 — Nickel Production Cost & HPM Revenue Analysis
"Act as a senior financial analyst for an Indonesian nickel mining operation. I will provide you with our current mining metrics: an actual Stripping Ratio (SR) of [insert, e.g., 3.8:1], an industrial diesel consumption rate, and a fixed overhead factor. Our target is extracting Saprolite ore with a grade of 1.7% Ni and 30% MC (Moisture Content). Help me calculate the projected total cash cost per Wet Metric Tonne (WMT). Then, cross-reference this against the current ESDM HPM (Harga Patokan Mineral) formula pricing to calculate our gross profit margin per tonne, including a sensitivity table showing the impact if fuel prices spike by 15%."
Prompt 32 — Nickel Fleet Productivity — Match Factor Calculation
"Act as a mining operations engineer at an Indonesian nickel pit. Calculate the Match Factor (MF) for our loading and hauling fleet using the standard formula: MF = (N_H × t_L) / (N_L × t_H). Where: Number of haul trucks (N_H) = [insert], Number of loaders (N_L) = [insert], Loader cycle time (t_L) = [insert], Truck total cycle time (t_H) = [insert]. Show your mathematical work step-by-step. Interpret the resulting MF value to tell me if our fleet suffers from loader bottlenecking or truck queuing, and suggest adjustments to optimize our hourly pit fuel consumption."
Prompt 34 — Nickel Stockpile Grade Blending for Smelter Feed
"Act as a Nickel Mine Quality Control (QC) Specialist. We need to ship a barge to a local smelter that strictly demands a blended grade of exactly 1.65% Ni. We have two separate stockpiles available at our jetty: Stockpile A contains 25,000 WMT of high-grade Saprolite at 1.85% Ni. Stockpile B contains 15,000 WMT of mid-grade ore at 1.40% Ni. Calculate the exact blending ratio and tonnage required from each stockpile to hit the 1.65% Ni target exactly, avoiding any smelter rejection or pricing penalties."
Prompt 37 — Nickel Ore Weight Correction — WMT to DMT
"Act as a nickel logistics accountant. Help me perform a dry weight correction calculation for a domestic bulk shipment to an Indonesian smelter. The wet metric tonnage (WMT) measured at our jetty weighbridge is 11,000 tonnes, with a Total Moisture (TM/MC) sample reading of 32%. Upon arrival at the buyer's smelter port, the independent surveyor measures a TM of 29%. Calculate the adjusted Dry Metric Tonnage (DMT) and the final target payable tonnage based on standard Indonesian nickel trading moisture adjustment rules."
Prompt 39 — Nickel Exploration Assay Data & Cut-off Grade Verification
"Create a step-by-step Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) checklist for our office-based geological team to verify laboratory assay results from a recent nickel laterite infill drilling campaign. Include QA/QC validation steps for checking Ni, Fe (Iron), and the Fe/Mg ratio variance margins of inserted blank samples, duplicates, and certified standards before updating our master resource block model to separate Saprolite from Limonite zones."
